# Problem

A company with a break-even point at P900,000 in sales revenue had fixed costs of P225,000. When actual sales were P1,000,000 variable costs were P750,000.

## Question 1

Determine the margin of safety expressed in pesos.

> [!success]- Solution
> **100,000** is the correct answer. 
> Margin of Safety = Actual Sales - Break-Even Sales
> MOS = P1,000,000 - P900,000 = P100,000.

## Question 2

Determine the margin of safety expressed as a percentage of sales.

> [!success]- Solution
> **10%** is the correct answer. 
> Margin of Safety Percentage = Margin of Safety / Actual Sales
> MOS % = P100,000 / P1,000,000 = 0.10 or 10%.

## Question 3

Determine the contribution margin ratio.

> [!success]- Solution
> **25%** is the correct answer. 
> At Break-Even, Total Contribution Margin = Total Fixed Costs. 
> So, at P900,000 sales, CM is P225,000. 
> CM Ratio = CM / Sales = P225,000 / P900,000 = 0.25 or 25%.
> Alternatively using actual sales: CM = Sales - VC = P1,000,000 - P750,000 = P250,000. CM Ratio = 250,000 / 1,000,000 = 25%.

## Question 4

Determine the operating income.

> [!success]- Solution
> **25,000** is the correct answer. 
> Operating Income = Total Contribution Margin - Fixed Costs
> Operating Income = P250,000 (from actual sales) - P225,000 = P25,000.
